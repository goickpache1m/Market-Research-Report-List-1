<p><ul><li>On-demand</li><li>Prophylaxis</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The hemophilia treatment market is divided into two primary types: on-demand treatment and prophylaxis. On-demand treatment involves administering clotting factor concentrates during bleeding episodes to manage acute situations, providing immediate relief. In contrast, prophylaxis is a preventive strategy that involves regular infusions of clotting factors to reduce the frequency and severity of bleeding episodes over time. Both approaches aim to enhance the quality of life for hemophilia patients, but they differ in their application and treatment goals.</p></p>

<p><p>The hemophilia treatment market encompasses various therapeutic approaches, including replacement therapy, immune tolerance induction (ITI) therapy, and gene therapy. ITI therapy aims to retrain the immune system to tolerate the infused factors, particularly in patients with inhibitors. Gene therapy represents a transformative approach, delivering functional copies of the clotting factor gene to enable the body to produce it independently, potentially offering long-term therapeutic solutions.</p></p>
