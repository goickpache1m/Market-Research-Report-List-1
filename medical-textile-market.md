<p><p>The medical textile market comprises three main types: non-woven, knitted, and woven textiles. Non-woven medical textiles are created by bonding fibers through processes like heat or chemicals, making them ideal for hygiene products such as surgical gowns and masks. Knitted medical textiles feature interlaced yarns that offer stretchability and comfort, commonly used in wound dressings and orthopedic supports. Woven medical textiles consist of interlaced threads, providing durability and structure, often employed in surgical fabrics and sutures, catering to diverse healthcare applications.</p></p>
