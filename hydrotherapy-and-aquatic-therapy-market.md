<p><p>Hydrotherapy and aquatic therapy are therapeutic practices that utilize water for rehabilitation and wellness. These therapies utilize the properties of water, such as buoyancy, viscosity, and temperature, to enhance physical recovery, relieve pain, and promote relaxation. Hydrotherapy typically involves the use of water in various forms, including baths, whirlpools, and steam, while aquatic therapy takes place in a pool environment, allowing patients to perform exercises and activities with reduced strain on their bodies.</p><p>The Hydrotherapy and Aquatic Therapy Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 11.8% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by an increasing awareness of the benefits of water-based therapy for rehabilitation, rising incidences of musculoskeletal disorders, and the growing demand for alternative and complementary therapies. Additionally, advancements in aquatic therapy equipment, such as hydrotherapy pools and advanced therapeutic devices, are further enhancing treatment efficacy. The integration of these therapies in rehabilitation centers, physiotherapy clinics, and wellness centers is also contributing to market expansion.
